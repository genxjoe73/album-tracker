Released June 13, 2014 by Polydor and Interscope, *Ultraviolence* is Lana Del Rey's third studio album and the deliberate sonic break from *Born to Die*. Where *Born to Die* (2012) was hip-hop-influenced trip-pop with prominent string arrangements and trap-adjacent percussion, *Ultraviolence* is a slow, narcotic, guitar-driven record built around analog production warmth — a near-total inversion of her commercial breakthrough's sound.

The album was produced by **Dan Auerbach** (of The Black Keys) at his Easy Eye Sound studio in Nashville. Auerbach's production approach — recording mostly to tape, building songs around bass and drums with extensive reverb and minimal layering — is responsible for the album's distinctive sonic character: dry vocals over wet rhythm, lots of tremolo guitar, very little contemporary pop polish. Del Rey has said she wrote much of the album in Nashville while collaborating with Auerbach in person rather than the long-distance Email-and-Logic-Pro workflow that produced earlier records.

Critically, *Ultraviolence* is often cited as the moment Del Rey's "haters" lost the argument — the album's sustained ambition and willingness to abandon her commercial signature won over critics who had dismissed *Born to Die* as a calculated industry product. Pitchfork moved her score from a 5.5 (*Born to Die*) to an 8.0 (*Ultraviolence*); the album hit #1 on the US Billboard 200 in its first week.

Track D1 is officially titled *Fucked My Way Up to the Top* — a deliberately confrontational title that responded to the persistent industry narrative that Del Rey's career was somehow inauthentic. Most pressings censor it on the labels and inserts to "F***** My Way Up to the Top" or similar; some variants spell it out fully on the back-cover credits. The 2014 first press of this catno (B0020950-01) censors with asterisks; a parallel 2014 variant (B0020952-01) spells it out on the insert.

**About this 2014 US first press (2×LP):** Polydor/Interscope catalog `B0020950-01`, US-pressed (notes say "Made in the USA" under the credits). The runouts are simple etched marks (`B002095001-A G1 IS` etc.) — the `G1 IS` suggests the original cutting plant. Comes with a color insert containing production credits.

The 2014 first press is the original Auerbach-mastered cut. Later variants include a Czech Republic re-press (r26267261, same catno, no Made in USA mark) and a 2021 Canadian reissue (r28644541) that was re-cut at Masterdisk by Scott Hull (`MASTERDISK SH` in the runout). For the original sonic intent, the 2014 US first press is the canonical pressing.

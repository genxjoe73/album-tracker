Directed by Adrian Maben, *Pink Floyd: Live at Pompeii* is a groundbreaking 1972 concert film shot in the empty ancient Roman amphitheater of Pompeii. Performing without an audience, the band runs through searing, extended versions of their pre-*Dark Side* progressive material, most notably "Echoes." It stands as the ultimate visual document of Pink Floyd's transition from 1960s psychedelic pioneers into the architects of 1970s progressive rock.

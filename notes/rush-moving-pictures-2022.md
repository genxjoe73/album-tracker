**Moving Pictures** is Rush's eighth studio album, released in February 1981 on Mercury Records, and their commercial and artistic peak. It opens with "Tom Sawyer" — one of the definitive rock tracks of the decade — and never lets up: "Red Barchetta," "YYZ," "Limelight," "The Camera Eye," "Witch Hunt," and "Vital Signs" across seven tracks that demonstrate the band's extraordinary technical command and compositional ambition. The album reached #3 on the Billboard 200 and is certified 4× platinum in the US.

Neil Peart's drumming on *Moving Pictures* is the benchmark by which progressive rock drumming is measured. Geddy Lee's bass, synth, and vocal work alongside Alex Lifeson's guitar create a sound that is immediately identifiable and impossible to replicate. The album is lean by prog standards — no track outstays its welcome — and the production by Terry Brown is crisp and powerful.

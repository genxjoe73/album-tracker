Released September 2015, *Honeymoon* is Lana Del Rey's fourth studio album and the most stylistically consistent of her early-to-middle period — slow, orchestral, languid, with a deliberate avoidance of the radio-friendly hooks that anchored *Born to Die* and *Ultraviolence*. Del Rey co-produced with Rick Nowels and Kieron Menzies (the *Ultraviolence* team minus Dan Auerbach), and the album was recorded primarily at The Green Building in Santa Monica.

The aesthetic is intentional: Del Rey has described *Honeymoon* as her "most cinematic" record, and the arrangements lean into film-noir strings, woozy reverb, and tempos that rarely break out of ballad territory. *High by the Beach* is the most up-tempo moment; the rest of the album sits in *Music to Watch Boys To* / *Terrence Loves You* / *Salvatore* mode — jazz-influenced, slow-burning, deliberately patient. The album includes a spoken-word reading of T.S. Eliot's *Burnt Norton* as track 6, which is either pretentious or perfect depending on how you feel about that kind of thing.

Critical reception was strong but quieter than her earlier records — *Honeymoon* is the album most often cited by Lana fans as "the one that grew on me." It's also the last fully Nowels-and-Menzies-produced Del Rey album before Jack Antonoff entered the picture for *Norman Fucking Rockwell!* — making the production lineage in this collection (Nowels/Menzies on *Honeymoon*, Antonoff on *NFR!*) trace the actual hinge of her career.

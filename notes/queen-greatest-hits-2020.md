**Greatest Hits** is Queen's first and most essential compilation, originally released in October 1981 on EMI Records — and the best-selling album in UK chart history. It collects the defining singles from their commercial peak: "Bohemian Rhapsody," "Killer Queen," "We Are the Champions," "We Will Rock You," "Somebody to Love," "Don't Stop Me Now," "Crazy Little Thing Called Love," "Another One Bites the Dust," and more. Seventeen tracks, zero filler — it functions as a perfect introduction to one of rock's greatest bands and holds up as a standalone listen on its own terms.

Freddie Mercury's vocal range and theatrical command, Brian May's unmistakable layered guitar orchestrations, John Deacon's melodic bass work, and Roger Taylor's driving drums made Queen genuinely unique — a hard rock band with operatic ambition and pop instincts who never repeated themselves across albums.

The **2020 half-speed remaster** (Hollywood / UMe catalog **D003654901**) was mastered at **Abbey Road Studios, Room 30** using the half-speed process — the same facility and process used on the 2022 Police Greatest Hits in this collection. Matrix codes `1322290 / 1893285E1` confirm this pressing. Half-speed mastering at Abbey Road is the gold standard for modern reissue quality, and this Queen pressing is among the best-regarded modern vinyl editions of the catalog.

**II** is the second studio album by Boyz II Men, released on **Motown Records** in August 1994. Produced primarily by **Jimmy Jam and Terry Lewis** (Flyte Tyme Productions) and **Babyface** (ECAF Productions), it arrived on the back of the group's breakthrough with *Cooleyhighharmony* and the smash single "End of the Road." *II* became one of the best-selling R&B albums of the 1990s, propelled by two singles that each broke the record for longest run at number one on the Billboard Hot 100 at the time of their chart peaks: **"I'll Make Love To You"** (14 weeks) and **"On Bended Knee"** (6 weeks, displacing "I'll Make Love To You" from the top spot — making Boyz II Men the first act to replace themselves at number one since The Beatles). The album sold over 12 million copies in the US and won two Grammy Awards.

The **2016 reissue** was released by **Motown Records / Universal Music Enterprises** under catalog **B0025089-01**, distributed by Universal Music Distribution. It was pressed as a **2×LP** on standard black vinyl, making it the more common domestic variant of this reissue (a companion blue vinyl pressing, r18261367, was issued simultaneously). The pressing plant is not explicitly identified on the release, but the `S-97847` stamper code in the deadwax is consistent with UMe's domestic pressing suppliers during this period. The `B-0025089-01A` deadwax inscription mirrors the catalog number with a side designation suffix.

The 14-track sequence across four sides spreads the album's content evenly, with the two signature ballads appearing on Side C ("I'll Make Love To You") and Side D ("On Bended Knee") — a configuration that mirrors their original album sequence and places the commercial centerpieces on the second disc.

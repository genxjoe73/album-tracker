Released March 15, 2024 by Interscope Records and MCA Nashville, *Deeper Well* is Kacey Musgraves' fifth studio album and a deliberate retreat from the synth-and-strings pop ambition of *Star-Crossed* (2021). Recorded primarily at Electric Lady Studios in New York with Daniel Tashian and Ian Fitchuk co-producing — the same team behind *Golden Hour* — the album leans on acoustic textures, slower tempos, and a meditative folk-influenced palette. Musgraves has framed it as a record about peace and slowing down, written largely after she moved out of Nashville and into a more rural setting.

The lead single *Deeper Well* and *Cardinal* anchor the album's emotional center — the former is a meditation on relationship boundaries (the well as a metaphor for needing more depth than circumstances allow), the latter is a song about a friend's passing. The whole record sits in a softer dynamic register than her earlier work; even the more upbeat tracks (*Heaven Is*, *Dinner with Friends*) avoid the shimmer of *Golden Hour*. *Deeper Well* won the Grammy for Best Country Album at the 2025 ceremony, Musgraves' second win in that category after *Golden Hour*.

The album's production design is intentionally low-resolution in a positive sense — lots of room sound, minimal stereo widening, vocals close to the listener with the band recorded as if in a single room. It's the album most often cited by Musgraves fans as her "Sunday morning" record.

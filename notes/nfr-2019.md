Released in August 2019, *Norman Fucking Rockwell!* is widely considered Lana Del Rey's magnum opus and one of the defining albums of the 2010s. Produced largely with Jack Antonoff, the album strips away the heavy orchestration of her earlier work in favor of lush, Laurel Canyon-inspired psych-rock, classic piano ballads, and folk overtones. It captures a distinctly Californian atmosphere, blending tragic romance with a cynical but affectionate view of the collapsing American dream.

The album's title is a wry reference to the iconic American painter Norman Rockwell, setting the tone for songs that explore the messy reality behind idealized American life. Tracks like "Venice Bitch," a nearly 10-minute psychedelic jam, and the deeply poignant "The Greatest," showcase Del Rey's sharpened songwriting and Antonoff's understated production. It earned widespread critical acclaim, cementing Del Rey's legacy as one of her generation's greatest lyricists.

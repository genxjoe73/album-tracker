**Meddle** is Pink Floyd's sixth studio album, released in October 1971 on Harvest Records, and one of the pivotal records in their transition from the Syd Barrett-era psychedelia toward the progressive ambition that would culminate in *The Dark Side of the Moon*. Side one contains five shorter tracks in various moods — the funky "One of These Days," the pastoral "A Pillow of Winds," the bluesy "Fearless," the oddity "San Tropez," and the doomy "Seamus." Side two is given entirely to "Echoes," the 23-minute suite that remains one of the greatest extended pieces in rock: a slowly evolving exploration of texture, dynamics, and atmosphere that anticipates DSOTM in every way.

*Meddle* is often underrated in the Pink Floyd canon precisely because it sits between the experimental early work and the blockbuster later albums, but "Echoes" alone places it among their finest achievements. The album was self-produced by the band, recorded at AIR Studios and Morgan Studios in London, and represents the band working at peak creative cohesion before commercial pressure took hold.

**Paradise Theatre** is Styx's tenth studio album, released in January 1981 on A&M Records, and their commercial peak — the first album in history to be certified platinum before its release date. It reached #1 on the Billboard 200 and produced the massive hits "The Best of Times" and "Too Much Time on My Hands," alongside the deeper cuts "Rockin' the Paradise," "She Cares," and "Half-Penny, Two Penny." The album is a concept record set in a fictional Chicago theatre, and the gatefold packaging reflects that theatrical ambition.

Styx in 1981 were operating at the intersection of arena rock, progressive ambition, and pure pop songcraft — Dennis DeYoung and Tommy Shaw trading lead vocals across a remarkably varied tracklist. The album's success made them one of the defining bands of the early MTV era, even before MTV launched.

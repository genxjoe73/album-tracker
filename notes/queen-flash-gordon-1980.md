**Flash Gordon (Original Soundtrack Music)** is Queen's soundtrack to the 1980 Mike Hodges sci-fi film of the same name, released in December 1980 on Elektra Records. It's a unique entry in the Queen catalog — more a collection of orchestral cues, sound design, and incidental pieces than a conventional album, interspersed with fully realized rock tracks. "Flash" (the theme) is one of their most recognizable songs, and "Flash's Theme (Reprise)" closes the album triumphantly. The record showcases Queen's ability to operate in cinematic territory without losing their identity.

The album occupies an odd but beloved place in the Queen canon — fans of the film regard it with deep affection, and "Flash" itself has become a stadium anthem. The interplay between Brian May's guitar and the orchestral arrangements throughout is genuinely inventive.

This is an **original 1980 US pressing** on Elektra Records (catalog **5E-518**). The deadwax `SP ESR 1-1 SM7-2 R-153474` identifies this as an early pressing — `SP` indicates Specialty Records pressing, `ESR` is the cutting engineer. A true first pressing of a cult Queen record.

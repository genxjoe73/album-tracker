**Sublime** is the self-titled third and final studio album by Sublime, released posthumously in July 1996 on Gasoline Alley / MCA Records — two months after frontman Bradley Nowell died of a heroin overdose at age 28. It became one of the best-selling albums of the 1990s, certified 5× platinum, and produced the defining alt-rock/reggae crossover hits "What I Got," "Santeria," "Wrong Way," "Doin' Time," and "Garden Grove." The album is a genre-blending masterpiece that fused punk, reggae, hip-hop, and ska into something entirely original and still sounds fresh today.

Produced by Paul Leary (Butthole Surfers) and the band's own David Kahne, the album has a loose, sun-soaked California feel that belies how tightly constructed the songs actually are. Nowell's guitar, vocals, and songwriting were singular, and the album stands as both a career peak and a monument to a talent lost far too soon.

**1** is the Beatles' best-selling compilation, released in November 2000 on Apple Records. The title refers to the criterion for inclusion: every track on the record reached number one on the UK Singles Chart, the US Billboard Hot 100, or both. Twenty-seven songs appear, spanning "Love Me Do" (1962) through "The Ballad of John and Yoko" (1969), making it a near-complete survey of the band's dominance of the singles charts across their active decade. It was the best-selling album worldwide in 2000 and has remained one of the top-selling compilations in the history of recorded music.

The **2015 reissue** was prompted by the preparation of new stereo remixes produced by **Giles Martin** — son of the Beatles' original producer George Martin — in collaboration with Sam Okell at Abbey Road. These remixes used the original multitrack session tapes and represent a thorough reworking of the stereo field for the pre-*Rubber Soul* material in particular, much of which existed only in primitive two-channel form on the original releases. Tracks A1 through A3 ("Love Me Do," "From Me To You," "She Loves You") retain the original mono mixes, as the stereo sources for those recordings were not considered viable for the remix treatment. Everything from "I Want To Hold Your Hand" onward is a 2015 Giles Martin stereo remix. The reissue was also issued in a companion 2×Blu-ray edition pairing audio with promotional films, directed by Apple Corps.

**Miles Showell** mastered this pressing at **Abbey Road Studios** using **half-speed mastering** — the lathe runs at half the standard 33⅓ RPM (i.e., 16⅔ RPM), which doubles the time the cutting stylus spends tracing each groove cycle. This extended contact time significantly improves the stylus's ability to reproduce high-frequency content, resulting in finer groove geometry, better transient definition, and a quieter noise floor compared to real-time cuts from the same source. Half-speed mastering has been Showell's signature method at Abbey Road and is widely regarded among audiophile collectors as producing the most faithful vinyl transfers of digital sources. His initials `Mile` appear in the deadwax alongside the `Abbey Road 1/2 Speed` inscription.

**Optimal Media GmbH** is a German pressing plant based in Röbel/Müritz and is one of the most respected large-scale vinyl manufacturers currently operating in Europe. Its plant code `BF06330` appears in the deadwax of this pressing — the `BF` prefix is an Optimal Media identifier. Optimal presses for major labels and audiophile imprints alike and is consistently regarded as producing quiet, well-centered pressings with good vinyl compound quality. A German Optimal pressing is a favorable configuration for a 2015 mainstream reissue.

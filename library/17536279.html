Hotel California

Production notes

Hotel California is the fourth studio album by the Eagles, released in December 1976 on Elektra/Asylum Records. It stands as one of the defining rock albums of the 1970s — produced by Bill Szymczyk (who also helmed the band’s previous multiplatinum One of These Nights), engineered by Glyn Johns, and recorded at Criteria Studios in Miami and The Record Plant in Los Angeles over a nine-month period. The title track, written by Don Felder, Don Henley, and Glenn Frey, became an iconic anthem with its haunting guitar intro (played by Felder on a 12-string acoustic) and ominous narrative. Joe Walsh’s lead guitar work on multiple tracks added textural complexity. The album spent a record-breaking 27 weeks at number one on the Billboard 200 and became one of the best-selling albums in American history — over 42 million copies worldwide, with 16 million in the US alone. It remains a cultural touchstone.

The 2017 reissue was part of a broader remaster campaign by Warner Music / Asylum Records, issued on 180-gram vinyl and marketed internationally. The pressing was made in the Netherlands (as stated on the back cover), and it includes a replica of the original Norman Seeff band photography poster from the 1976 debut. The catalog number RRM1-1084 is the remaster identifier (with the -A and -B designations indicating Side A and Side B). The barcode 081227961619 is the international EAN code.

Chris Bellman cut the lacquers for this pressing at The Mastering Lab in Los Angeles — evident from the matrix inscriptions. Bellman is one of the most acclaimed mastering engineers of the modern era, known for his meticulous approach to analog-to-digital and digital-to-analog transfers. His signature appears alongside the studio codes 1785902 and 956086a, which are internal production identifiers at The Mastering Lab. The SB etch is an individual engineer or quality-control mark. This pressing differs from the 2014 European variant (r6623165), which was cut by Bernie Grundman.

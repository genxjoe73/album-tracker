Production notes

Rumours is Fleetwood Mac’s eleventh studio album, released in February 1977 on Warner Bros. Records, and one of the best-selling albums in history — with over 40 million copies sold worldwide, it remains among the top five best-selling albums of all time. Recorded amid the personal turmoil of multiple simultaneous relationship breakups within the band (Buckingham/Nicks, McVie/McVie), the album channels that emotional upheaval into an extraordinarily polished suite of pop-rock songs. Every track connects — “Go Your Own Way,” “The Chain,” “Dreams,” “Gold Dust Woman,” “Don’t Stop,” “You Make Loving Fun” — and the album’s production, handled by the band with Cris Morris and Ken Caillat, set a new standard for studio craft in the late 1970s.

Rumours is particularly notable for the way it balances emotional rawness with sonic sophistication. The band’s internal chaos never disrupts the album’s surface perfection — but that surface perfection is precisely what makes the underlying tension so powerful. It is one of the defining artifacts of the California rock era and remains in regular rotation on streaming platforms decades after its release.

This 1977 original US Warner Bros. pressing (catalog BSK 3010) was pressed at Capitol Records’ pressing plant and mastered by Ken Perry (KP) — one of the most respected mastering engineers of the era, responsible for countless classic rock pressings. The matrix codes BSK-2-3010 JW6 F38 #6 MASTERED BY CAPITOL KP identify this as a JW stamper pressing, with the F38 indicating a later stamper generation from the original lacquer. Ken Perry’s Capitol mastering consistently delivered warm, dynamic pressings that hold up extremely well today.

About this 1977 US pressing (Vinyl, LP): Warner Bros. Records catalog BSK 3010, original 1977 US pressing. Mastered by Ken Perry (KP) at Capitol. JW stamper, F38 lacquer generation.

Production notes

Born to Die is the debut album by Lana Del Rey, released in January 2012 on Interscope Records. It introduces her distinctive aesthetic: cinematic arrangements, sampled orchestral strings, nostalgic melodies, and lyrics preoccupied with Americana, romance, melancholy, and mortality. Songs like “Video Games,” “National Anthem,” and the title track became defining works of the 2010s, establishing her as a major voice in alternative pop. The album was initially released as a major-label debut under her stage name (she had recorded earlier material under other identities), and it went platinum in multiple territories, selling over 5 million copies worldwide.

This red opaque vinyl reissue was released by Interscope / Polydor on catalog B0016425-01 (barcode 602577673771). The pressing includes a lyric sheet with photo on the reverse. The exact year of this pressing is not specified on Discogs, though the catalog structure and pressing plant suggest a reissue from the 2010s rather than original 2012 material.

MRP2259 is the mastering code assigned by Masterdisk — the renowned New York mastering facility that has handled thousands of major-label releases across genres. The matrix 199787E1/A1 indicates a mid-series lacquer cut. SH A18 and SH B27 are additional stamper or QC designations.

Gemini Notes

Released in 2012, Born to Die introduced Lana Del Rey’s cinematic, sad-core pop aesthetic to the mainstream. Blending sweeping orchestral arrangements with hip-hop beats and vintage Americana imagery, the album proved hugely influential on the decade’s pop landscape.

Production notes

Blurryface is Twenty One Pilots’ fourth studio album, released in May 2015 on Fueled by Ramen / Atlantic Records, and the record that broke the Columbus, Ohio duo — Tyler Joseph (vocals, keyboards, bass) and Josh Dun (drums) — into mainstream stardom. The album debuted at #1 on the Billboard 200 and produced the massive crossover hits “Stressed Out” and “Ride,” along with “Tear in My Heart” and “Lane Boy.” It is one of the rare albums where every track charted on the Billboard Hot 100 simultaneously. Blurryface reached 6× platinum in the US.

Thematically, the album is built around the alter ego “Blurryface” — a manifestation of Tyler Joseph’s insecurities — and explores anxiety, self-doubt, and identity with a sonic palette that blends alternative rock, hip-hop, reggae, and electronic production. Co-produced by Joseph with executive production by Ricky Reed and Chris Woltman, mixed by Neal Avron, the album’s eclectic genre-blending was a significant evolution from their earlier work and set the template for their subsequent commercial success.

This 2015 original European pressing was mastered by Chris Gehringer at Sterling Sound, New York — one of the foremost mastering engineers of the modern era. Lacquer cut by Martin Wegner at SST Brüggemann GmbH in Germany — the SST and MW (Martin Wegner’s initials) in the deadwax confirm this. Pressed by MPO in France, one of Europe’s most respected pressing plants. The die-cut sleeve includes a red clear plastic sheer insert that colors the black-and-white inner sleeve.

About this 2015 EU pressing (2× Vinyl, LP): Fueled by Ramen / WEA International catalog 7567-86696-3, original 2015 European pressing. Black vinyl. Mastered by Chris Gehringer at Sterling Sound. Lacquer cut by Martin Wegner (MW) at SST Brüggemann GmbH. Pressed by MPO, France. Barcode 075678669637. Matrix MPO 075678669637 A SST MW.

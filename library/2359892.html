Physical Graffiti

Production notes

Physical Graffiti is Led Zeppelin’s sixth studio album, released in February 1975 as a double LP on their own Swan Song Records label — the first release on the label they co-founded with Atlantic Records. At nearly 83 minutes across four sides, it is the band’s most expansive and arguably most ambitious statement, drawing from sessions spanning 1970 to 1974 and covering an extraordinary range of styles: hard rock (“Custard Pie,” “The Wanton Song”), acoustic folk (“Bron-Y-Aur,” “Tangerine” outtake), Eastern-influenced epic (“Kashmir”), blues (“In My Time of Dying”), and melodic rock (“Trampled Under Foot,” “Houses of the Holy”). “Kashmir” alone stands as one of the greatest recordings in rock history — a hypnotic, orchestral-inflected juggernaut that showcases the band at their most cinematic.

The album debuted at #1 in both the US and UK and arrived at the peak of Led Zeppelin’s commercial and creative powers. Its release caused such demand that all previous Zeppelin albums simultaneously re-entered the charts.

This 1975 original US pressing on Swan Song Records (catalog SS 2-200) was mastered at Sterling Sound (matrix prefix ST-SS) with F.T. (Fred Twomey, a Sterling engineer) cutting the lacquer, pressed at Presswell (PR) in New Jersey. The GD marking may identify a quality control inspector or secondary engineer. Sterling Sound masterings of mid-1970s Atlantic/Swan Song releases are consistently warm and dynamic, and this original pressing represents the album as it was first heard by American audiences in 1975.

About this 1975 US pressing (2× Vinyl, LP): Swan Song Records catalog SS 2-200, original 1975 US pressing. Double LP. Mastered at Sterling Sound (ST-SS), cut by F.T. (Fred Twomey), pressed at Presswell (PR). Matrix ST-SS-753310-E.

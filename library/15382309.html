Exit...Stage Left

Production notes

Exit…Stage Left is Rush’s second official live album and the document of the band at their commercial zenith. Recorded during the Moving Pictures tour in 1981 — the same cycle that had just produced the band’s breakthrough album and the era-defining “Tom Sawyer” — the recording captures a band operating at maximum density: Geddy Lee’s bass-and-Moog juggling act, Alex Lifeson’s layered electric-and-synthesizer approach, and Neil Peart’s percussion infrastructure were all at their most technically demanding. The setlist spans five albums: the three-part survey of Side A (“Spirit of Radio,” “Red Barchetta,” “YYZ”) opens with Moving Pictures’ top to bottom, the gatefold’s middle sides pull from Hemispheres, Permanent Waves, and the older catalog (“A Passage to Bangkok,” “Jacob’s Ladder,” “Xanadu”), and the closer Side D reprises Permanent Waves and Moving Pictures material before ending on the extended instrumental “La Villa Strangiato.” The result is not a greatest-hits survey but a coherent live argument for the band’s progressive ambitions, with the 1977–1981 catalog functioning as a unified body of work. Notably, the brief acoustic piece “Broon’s Bane” — Terry Brown’s nickname, rendered here by Lifeson — serves as a palette cleanser before “The Trees” and the sprawling “Xanadu,” a structurally smart move that gives Side C its own internal narrative arc.

The recording was a multi-unit operation. Terry Brown — who had produced Rush continuously since Fly by Night (1975) and co-produced the Moving Pictures sessions — served as engineer alongside Paul Northfield, who handled the mix. Sides A, C, and D were tracked in Canada using Le Mobile (Guy Charbonneau’s remote unit, also used on Permanent Waves) and the Record Plant Mobile, while Side B was tracked in the UK using Mobile One. The logistical split across two continents accounts for the hybrid metal-parts situation visible in the deadwax. Mastering was handled by Bob Ludwig, one of the most respected mastering engineers in North America during this period, through his work at Masterdisk.

On the pressing side: Mercury Records issued the album under catalog number SRM-2-7001, where SRM-2 designates a double-LP in Mercury’s standard US vinyl catalog series. For the US market, distribution of Mercury titles ran through Columbia’s pressing infrastructure. Columbia Records operated multiple pressing plants in the early 1980s — most commonly Hauppauge (HRM) in New York for initial pressings, and Terre Haute in Indiana and Carrollton, GA for continued stock. The Hauppauge/HRM plant is where most collectors expect a first pressing of this title; Carrollton is a repress variant reflecting Columbia’s regional distribution.

The deadwax on this copy tells a specific story. The plant identifier G1 — present on Sides A, C, and D — is Columbia’s code for the Carrollton, Georgia plant, confirming the physical pressing on three of four sides. Side B is the anomaly: the T1 marker in the runout (SRM2-7001B-1A XG T1 R6) indicates that the metal parts originated at Terre Haute, Columbia’s Indiana plant. The groove was pressed at Carrollton from a stamper shipped from Terre Haute — a hybrid pressing not uncommon when Columbia routed metal parts between facilities. The XG initials on Sides B and C represent an unidentified cutter, consistent with a Masterdisk-affiliated individual active in the early 1980s, though not definitively named in the collector literature.

About this 1981 US pressing (2×LP, gatefold): Mercury Records catalog SRM-2-7001, pressed at the Columbia Carrollton, GA plant (G1), with Side B using Terre Haute (T1) metal parts. Deadwax: Side A — G1 SRM-2-7001A-1C C 9; Side B — SRM2-7001B-1A XG T1 R6; Side C — G1 SRM-2-7001C-1B XG1 D2G; Side D — G1 SRM-2-7001D-1B A 19. Mastered by Bob Ludwig. Standard gatefold jacket with Deborah Samuel photography and Hugh Syme art direction. This copy carries a white CRC sticker on the back cover — CRC (Columbia Record Club) indicates distribution through Columbia’s mail-order club rather than retail, which is consistent with the Carrollton plant and the hybrid Terre Haute metal parts: Columbia House club pressings routinely drew from Carrollton stock using parts shipped from other facilities. The audio is identical to the retail pressing; the CRC designation is a distribution-channel distinction, not a sonic one.

Notes

Gatefold cover pressed at Columbia Carrollton pressing plant (identified by G1 in runouts). One side appears to use metal parts sourced from Columbia Terre Haute (T1 in runout). Labels printed with "18" which would normally indicate a Presswell pressing, but Columbia House typically used labels sourced from other pressing plants. Some copies have a white CRC sticker on back cover. All tracks recorded live during Rush's 1981 world tour. Sides A, C, and D were recorded in Canada by Le Mobile and the Record Plant Mobile. Side B was recorded in the UK by Mobile One.

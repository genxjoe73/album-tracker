Ummagumma

Production notes

Released October 1969, Ummagumma is a deliberate experiment: a double album split into one live LP and one studio LP, with the studio LP further split into four solo segments — one per band member, each writing and recording their own material independently. The live half was assembled from two performances at Mothers Club in Birmingham (April 1969) and the College of Commerce in Manchester (May 1969), edited together to present the band’s pre-Atom Heart Mother live repertoire.

The studio half was the band’s answer to a question they were posing to themselves: what does each of us actually contribute? Each member got a side-quarter to produce alone. Richard Wright wrote Sysyphus (a four-part keyboard piece). Roger Waters did Grantchester Meadows (acoustic, pastoral) and the famously strange Several Species of Small Furry Animals Gathered Together in a Cave and Grooving with a Pict — a tape collage of Waters making animal sounds and crawling around the studio, manipulated through varispeed. David Gilmour contributed the three-part The Narrow Way. Nick Mason — who doesn’t write — assembled The Grand Vizier’s Garden Party with help from his then-wife Lindy on flute.

The verdict on the studio half varies. Gilmour has called it “appalling.” Waters has been kinder. Most listeners agree it’s an interesting document of where each member’s instincts pointed before the band converged on the Meddle / Dark Side sound.

About this US Capitol pressing: the original 1969 US issue was on Harvest with catno STBB-388. Capitol re-cut the lacquers some time in the early 1970s and the catno prefix changed from STBB to SKBB-388 on the recut sleeves and labels — the runouts on those recuts often show SKBB / STBB̶ overstrikes (the cutter scribed the new prefix over the old) and a MASTERED BY CAPITOL stamp with a small triangle (–◁). The G/H + number codes in the runouts are Capitol cutting-engineer initials and cut numbers.

Catno on sleeve TBD — STBB-388 would be the early 1973 STBB repress (Discogs r444459); SKBB-388 would be the SKBB recut (Discogs r1039062, currently captured). Re-point if the sleeve says STBB.

Notes

Likely issued from 1970-1973 as front cover has "Special Buy!" Live Album recorded at Mothers Birmingham & Manchester College of Commerce, June 1969 Second studio album LP features multi-part experimental pieces each written by an individual band member. 6th pressing with SKBB-388 on both sleeve and Harvest label; Front sleeve: "Gigi" album cover leaning against wall is whited out; Text around bottom half edge of label starts at 10:00 position and states: "Mfd. by Capitol Records, Inc., a subsidiary of Capitol Industries-EMI, Inc., U.S.A. - Capitol Marca Reg. - All rights reserved. Unauthorized duplication is a violation of applicable laws." Next issue (7th pressing) split this text onto top and bottom half of label. Matrix inscriptions are etched, except for "MASTERED BY CAPITOL" which is stamped. Some parts of STBB in matrix inscriptions have T and K superimposed.

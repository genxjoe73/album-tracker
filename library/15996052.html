Production notes

Mariah Carey is the debut album by singer and songwriter Mariah Carey, released in June 1990 on Columbia Records. Produced primarily by Mariah Carey herself, Walter Afanasieff, and Brenda K. Starr, the album introduced her five-octave vocal range and whistle register to mainstream audiences. “Vision of Love” became her signature song and a defining moment in 1990s R&B and pop — the production, vocal arrangement, and the now-iconic music video established her as a major commercial force. The album went multiplatinum in multiple countries and won two Grammy Awards, including Best New Artist. It remains one of the best-selling debut albums by a female artist.

The 2020 reissue was undertaken as part of a broader reissue campaign by Columbia/Sony and Legacy Recordings. It was remastered at 24-bit Hi-Res Audio for vinyl, indicating a new transfer chain from high-resolution sources (likely the original mixdown masters or a 24/96 or 24/192 derivate) to the cutting lathe, in contrast to the standard 16-bit CD masters used for the original 1990 pressing. This represents a deliberate upgrade in fidelity for a contemporary collector-oriented edition.

GZ Media mastered this pressing (indicated by the MRP2793 cutting code, GZ’s internal identifier). GZ Media is a Czech-based mastering and pressing facility that serves major labels and is widely regarded as producing consistently high-quality vinyl. Memphis Record Pressing manufactured the physical record — a domestic US plant that has handled work for major labels and specialty labels alike.

About this 2020 US pressing (LP, black vinyl): Columbia / Legacy catalog 19439776361, remastered at 24-bit Hi-Res Audio for vinyl, mastered by GZ Media (code MRP2793), pressed by Memphis Record Pressing. Deadwax: 211088E1 / MRP2793 / 19439776361.

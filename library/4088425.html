Production notes

Led Zeppelin II is Led Zeppelin’s second studio album, released in October 1969 on Atlantic Records, and one of the most powerful rock records ever made. Recorded in fragments across multiple studios in the US and UK during the band’s relentless touring schedule, it captures the band at their most primal and ferocious — “Whole Lotta Love,” “Heartbreaker,” “Ramble On,” “Moby Dick,” and “Bring It On Home” form a template for hard rock and heavy metal that has never been surpassed. Jimmy Page’s production is dense and visceral, John Bonham’s drums hit with physical force, and Robert Plant’s vocal performance is raw in a way he never quite replicated.

The album went to #1 in both the US and UK and fundamentally shaped the next decade of rock music. Its influence on heavy metal, hard rock, and blues-rock is incalculable. “Whole Lotta Love” in particular — with its psychedelic breakdown, Page’s studio manipulation, and Plant’s barely contained performance — remains one of the most electrifying recordings in rock history.

This 1969 original US pressing on Atlantic Records (catalog SD 8236) is the real thing — pressed within weeks of the album’s release, mastered at Sterling Sound (SS) with RL (Bob Ludwig or Ron Lorman) cutting engineer credits in the deadwax. Sterling Sound was Atlantic’s primary mastering facility in this era, and these original Atlantic pressings are widely regarded as the definitive sonic documents of Led Zeppelin’s early catalog. The matrix ST-A-691671 identifies Sterling Sound’s job number for this album, and the stamper suffix places your copy in the broader run of original pressings from this lacquer.

Notes

US 1st pressing, featuring Robert Ludwig's "hot" mastering on both sides. These were withdrawn early because the high bass levels caused low-grade cartridges to mistrack, leading people to return the album on the belief that it was defective. Another engineer was then hired to recut a much less "hot" version of the album, and that is the far more common version. Labels include "MO" suffix (indicating Monarch pressing) and "Mfg By Atlantic Recording Corp., 1841 Broadway, New York, N.Y.". On all matrix Side A and Side B there is "MR" in a circle that identify a Monarch pressing. Some copies came with one or two hype stickers: one mentioning Whole Lotta Love and the other with a tracklisting. Runout details are etched except for the Monarch logo (MR in a circle) stamp. LW etching appears sideways compared to other text.

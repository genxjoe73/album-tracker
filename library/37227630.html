Middle Of Nowhere

Production notes

Released May 2026, Middle of Nowhere is Kacey Musgraves’ sixth studio album and her first on Lost Highway Records — a deliberate move back to a more traditional country label after the pop-leaning Star-Crossed (2021) and Deeper Well (2024). Musgraves has described the album as feeling like “a big circle” back to her early-career roots in Same Trailer Different Park and Pageant Material: country songwriting in the storytelling tradition rather than the cosmic-pop tradition Golden Hour opened up.

The album was recorded primarily in Nashville with Daniel Tashian and Ian Fitchuk co-producing — the same production team behind Golden Hour, but applied here to a more straightforwardly country palette. Pedal steel, fiddle, and acoustic textures dominate; the synth-and-strings layering of Star-Crossed is largely absent.

About this US Limited Edition pressing (Brown Translucent “Whiskey” with signed insert): the lacquer was cut by Joe Nino-Hernes at Sterling Sound (JN-H STERLING in the runout) — the same engineer who cut Lana Del Rey’s Norman Fucking Rockwell! 50th Anniversary Pallas pressing in your collection. Nino-Hernes is one of the current top cutting engineers for major-label country and pop releases. The cut numbers in the runout (313070E1 / 313070E2 for the lacquers, plus the 02496658 / 02493657 family of cut-pair identifiers) are Sterling’s job-tracking codes.

This pressing is the “Whiskey” colored variant — brown translucent vinyl, limited edition with a signed insert. The standard black vinyl issue is r37270545; the standard color (no signature) Whiskey issue is r37209792. Your specific copy with the signed insert is r37227630.

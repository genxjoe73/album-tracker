Production notes

Recorded April–December 1976 at Britannia Row Studios in Islington — the band’s own newly-built studio, replacing the earlier Abbey Road sessions. The album was assembled under significant pressure: the band had become tax exiles, internal tensions were rising, and Roger Waters has since described the original mix as rushed.

Columbia’s US issue (catno JC 34474) was pressed at three plants depending on regional distribution: Santa Maria, Pitman, or Terre Haute. The deadwax stamps tell you which — 1T indicates Terre Haute; absence of a plant marker typically points to Pitman or Santa Maria. Mastering on the 1977 US cuts is generally credited to Greg Calbi at Sterling Sound, with some pressings cut by George Marino.

Analog cut from analog tape, no digital intermediate. This is the historical document of the original mix as released — the version that existed before the 2018 remix reframed the album.

Gemini Notes

Released in 1977, Animals is Pink Floyd’s aggressive, synthesizer-heavy adaptation of George Orwell’s Animal Farm. It serves as a stark bridge between the spaciousness of Wish You Were Here and the theatricality of The Wall.

The Dark Side Of The Moon

Production notes

Released in March 1973, The Dark Side of the Moon is Pink Floyd’s commercial breakthrough and arguably the defining album of the progressive rock era. Recorded at Abbey Road Studios with engineer Alan Parsons, the album explores themes of time, greed, conflict, and mental illness, partly inspired by the departure of founding member Syd Barrett. Its seamless transitions, use of musique concrète, philosophical lyrics, and groundbreaking synthesizer work created a cohesive auditory journey that changed how concept albums were made.

The album’s legendary status is matched by its astonishing chart performance, remaining on the Billboard 200 for over 700 consecutive weeks. Iconic elements—such as the cash register loop in “Money,” Clare Torry’s wordless vocal improvisation on “The Great Gig in the Sky,” and the heartbeat that frames the record—have embedded themselves into modern cultural consciousness.

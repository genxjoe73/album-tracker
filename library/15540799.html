Production notes

The Wall is Pink Floyd’s eleventh studio album, released in November 1979 as a double LP. The record was co-produced by Roger Waters, Bob Ezrin, David Gilmour, and Nick Mason, with Ezrin’s orchestral and theatrical instincts shaping the album’s dense, layered sonic architecture. Mixed by James Guthrie, who had joined the band’s inner circle during the recording sessions at Britannia Row, Super Bear, Miravel, CBS, and Cherokee Studios, The Wall remains one of the most technically ambitious rock productions of its era. The conceptual arc — a rock star named Pink constructing a psychological wall against the world — unfolds across four sides of vinyl with an almost operatic sense of pacing, culminating in the mock-trial finale of “The Trial.” Commercially, the album was enormous, spending fifteen weeks at number one in the United States and producing the band’s only US chart-topping single, “Another Brick in the Wall, Part II.” It is, alongside Dark Side of the Moon, the axis around which Pink Floyd’s legacy turns.

The mastering lineage of The Wall on vinyl is largely the story of James Guthrie’s continuing custodianship of the recording. Guthrie mixed the original album and has remained the primary steward of its audio across subsequent reissues. In 2016, Pink Floyd Records initiated a comprehensive remaster of The Wall, with Guthrie working alongside Joel Plante — the same two-person team responsible for the 2018 Animals remix and remaster. The resulting 2016 remaster (catalogued on Discogs as r8957729) became the audio source for all subsequent authorized vinyl pressings under the Pink Floyd Records umbrella. For this pressing, the lacquers were cut by Bernie Grundman, whose initials BG appear in the deadwax and whose Beverly Hills mastering studio is one of the most respected cutting rooms in North America. Grundman’s involvement signals a deliberate audiophile-tier cut rather than a mechanical duplication of an earlier lacquer set.

About this 2018 US repress (2×LP, 180g, gatefold): Pink Floyd Records catalog PFRLP11, barcode 888751842816, pressed at Record Industry in Haarlem, Netherlands — one of Europe’s premier pressing plants, identifiable in the deadwax by the plant job code 18009. The full matrix reads 18009 xX 88875184281-X BG: the 18009 prefix is the Record Industry plant stamp; 88875184281 is the matrix base tied to the 2016 Pink Floyd Records remaster; and BG confirms the Bernie Grundman lacquer cut. The audio source is the same 2016 Guthrie/Plante remaster as the 2016 first pressing (r8957729), making this a sonically equivalent variant distinguished primarily by its lacquer generation and plant run. Physically, the record is 180-gram vinyl in the original gatefold jacket with the Gerald Scarfe artwork intact. Side 4 is notably shorter than the other three — a consequence of the album’s sequencing, with the brief closing track “Outside the Wall” landing after the cathartic peak of “The Trial.” The pressing is US-market configured despite Netherlands manufacture, reflecting the common practice of European plants supplying North American catalog pressings for Pink Floyd Records releases during this period.
